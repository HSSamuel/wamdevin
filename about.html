Who We Are

A legacy of strengthening public administration across West Africa since 1987.

Brief History

Olaolu A. Adewumi

Executive Secretary

"Building a stronger institution capable of delivering on its mandate to member countries."

WAMDEVIN originated from a Commonwealth initiative during the era of UN-driven globalisation, when member governments tasked the Commonwealth Secretariat (COMSEC) with creating sub-regional capacity-building agencies. These agencies were meant to strengthen Management Development Institutes (MDIs) so they could better train public servants in governance, skills, and leadership.

Following similar bodies created in the 1980s—such as AMTIESA in East/Southern Africa and AMDISA in Asia—a Roundtable of West African MDI leaders and policymakers was convened at ASCON, Nigeria, in November 1987 by COMSEC, EDI, and the ILO. The meeting aimed to promote collaboration among MDIs in the sub-region. A key outcome was the establishment of the West African Management Development and Training Institutions Network (WAMDEVIN) as a professional, non-profit network. Its first Secretariat was based at GIMPA in Ghana.

In 1990, WAMDEVIN’s Executive Committee approved relocating the Permanent Secretariat to the Administrative Staff College of Nigeria (ASCON), supported by the Nigerian government due to ASCON’s capacity, infrastructure, and expertise.

Today, WAMDEVIN is a sub-regional network of MDIs and business schools focused on human resource development, consultancy, research, and publications across Anglophone West Africa—namely Nigeria, Ghana, Sierra Leone, The Gambia, Liberia, and Cameroon.

Our Mission

To harness the enormous potentials which member institutions could derive from pooling and sharing of limited expertise in management development, training, research and consultancy.

Our Vision

To be a World Class West African Network of Management Development Institutes positively shaping public policy and management through learning, research, and innovation.

Strategic Objectives

Our core functions designed to foster regional growth.

Experience Sharing

Creating a forum for sharing institutional experience on management development.

Capacity Building

Promoting skills development among professionals involved in administration.

Research & Policy

Conducting research to serve as think-tanks for government policy formulation.

Resource Development

Cooperating in the production and sharing of teaching and training materials.

Information Exchange

Disseminating relevant information through newsletters, databanks, and publications.

Staff Exchange

Strengthening institutional capabilities through faculty staff exchange programs.
