Research & Development

Driving policy innovation through rigorous data analysis and field studies.

Advancing Evidence-Based Governance

At WAMDEVIN, we believe that sustainable development in West Africa must be anchored on empirical research. Our R&D division collaborates with member institutes to dissect complex administrative challenges and propose data-driven solutions.

From analyzing public expenditure efficiency to studying leadership behaviors in the civil service, our research outputs serve as the intellectual foundation for policy reforms across the sub-region.

Thematic Focus Areas

Our research agenda targets the most pressing issues in African public administration.

Entrepreneurship & Employment

Assessing the impact of university entrepreneurship programmes on reducing graduate unemployment.

The Informal Sector

Analyzing the dynamics of the informal economy to integrate it into national development strategies.

Food Security

Policy research on agricultural sustainability and food distribution networks across West Africa.

Human Advancement

Studies focused on improving the Human Development Index (HDI) through public service interventions.

Child Labour & Trafficking

Investigating the root causes and policy gaps regarding child exploitation in the sub-region.

Institutional Collaboration

Researching models for effective partnerships between MDIs, governments, and donor agencies.

From Data to Policy

Our rigorous approach ensures research translates into actionable change.

Field Investigation

We deploy cross-border teams to gather primary data from public institutions and citizens.

Analysis & Review

Data is subjected to rigorous statistical analysis and peer review by academic experts.

Policy Advocacy

Findings are published in policy briefs and presented to governments for implementation.
